Travel to Vietnam

Explore the country as you admire the stunning landscapes, free your soul on white sandy beaches, and
experience the unique and beautiful culture that is found in Vietnam. Be sure to also join in on outstanding
mega culture, music, sports, events and festivals, and of course, enjoy the delicious Vietnamese cuisine.

Events and Festivities

Tết (Vietnamese New Year)

If you're in the area during the New Year, called Tết, Hoi An is transformed into a kaleidoscope of colour and
light for its Lantern Festival. The celebration lasts for seven days, with the road from Hoi An Bridge to the
Hoai River Square adorned with thousands of colourful lanterns. The heart of the festivities is in the old town,
between the Japanese Covered Bridge and the Cau An Hoi Bridge. It’s crowded, chaotic and festive, with
spontaneous singing and food stalls at every turn. It is as much a celebration for locals as it is for visitors.
There are also smaller lantern festivals held on each full moon.

Mid-Autumn Festival

This popular festival belongs to the children but is still fun for adults too. With ancient roots rich in myth and
legend, travellers will delight in seeing kids performing lion dances and participating in street processions.
It’s also customary to give and receive Banh Trung Thu, or boxes of mooncakes, during this time. Filled with
lotus seeds, red beans and sometimes a yoke from a salted egg to represent the moon, don’t miss tasting this
traditional festival food.
